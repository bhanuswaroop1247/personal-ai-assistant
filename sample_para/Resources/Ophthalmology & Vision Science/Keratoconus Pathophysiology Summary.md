# Keratoconus Pathophysiology and Progression Indicators

**Type:** Knowledge Reference | **Domain:** Ophthalmology – Corneal Disease  
**Last Updated:** 2025

---

## What Is Keratoconus?

Keratoconus (KC) is a progressive, non-inflammatory ectatic disorder of the cornea. The corneal stroma thins and biomechanically weakens, leading to forward bulging (ectasia), irregular astigmatism, and eventually significant visual impairment if untreated.

- **Prevalence:** ~1 in 2000 in the general population; up to 1 in 500 in some regions.
- **Onset:** Typically second or third decade of life.
- **Progression:** Episodic in some patients; accelerated by eye rubbing, atopy, contact lens wear.

---

## Structural Changes in the Cornea

### Stromal Thinning
The hallmark of KC is inferior or inferotemporal corneal thinning. The thinnest point migrates from the center over time.

### Bowman's Layer Disruption
Breaks in Bowman's layer allow keratocyte migration; this is one of the earliest histological markers. Fibrotic scars (subepithelial fibrosis) appear at later stages.

### Keratocyte Apoptosis
Loss of keratocytes in the anterior stroma reduces structural integrity. Thought to be triggered by mechanical stress from eye rubbing and oxidative damage.

### Collagen Fibril Disorganization
Normally orthogonally arranged collagen fibrils in the stroma become irregular in KC, reducing tensile strength (demonstrated by X-ray diffraction studies).

## Key Clinical / Topographic Parameters

| Parameter | Normal Range | KC Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Kmax (max simK) | < 47.2 D | > 47.2 D (suspicious) |
| Kmax - Kmin asymmetry | < 1.5 D | > 2 D |
| Thinnest Pachymetry | > 490 µm | < 470 µm |
| BSCVA | 20/20 | Reduced with progression |
| BAD-D (Belin/Ambrósio) | < 1.6 | > 1.6 (suspicious) |
| ISV (Index of Surface Variance) | Low | Elevated |
| IHD (Index of Height Decentration) | < 0.014 | Elevated |

## Grading Systems

### Amsler-Krumeich Classification
Stage I–IV based on keratometry and refraction:
- **Stage I:** K < 48 D, BCVA ≥ 20/40
- **Stage II:** K < 53 D
- **Stage III:** K < 55 D, central opacity absent
- **Stage IV:** K > 55 D or central opacity present

### ABCD Grading (Belin/Ambrósio, Pentacam)
More nuanced 4-parameter system incorporating anterior/posterior elevation, minimum thickness, and CDVA.

## Risk Factors for Progression

- Young age (< 20 at diagnosis = higher risk)
- Chronic eye rubbing (mechanical trauma theory)
- Atopic disease (allergic eye disease comorbidity)
- Family history (autosomal dominant component with variable penetrance)
- Contact lens wear (confounds topography; also associated with progression in some studies)

---

## Treatment Ladder

1. **Spectacles / Soft CLs** — early stage, irregular astigmatism < 2.5 D
2. **Rigid Gas Permeable / Scleral Lenses** — moderate stage, vault over cone
3. **Corneal Cross-Linking (CXL)** — gold standard to halt progression; creates additional covalent bonds between collagen fibrils via riboflavin + UV-A
4. **Intrastromal Ring Segments (ICRS)** — improve optics, reduce progression
5. **Keratoplasty (DALK/PK)** — end-stage, scar, or contact lens intolerance
