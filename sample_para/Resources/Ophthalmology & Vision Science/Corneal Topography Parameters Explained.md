# Corneal Topography Parameters Explained

**Type:** Knowledge Reference | **Domain:** Ophthalmology – Diagnostic Imaging  
**Last Updated:** 2025

---

## Overview

Corneal topography maps the surface curvature of the anterior (and in modern devices, posterior) cornea. It is the primary diagnostic tool for detecting keratoconus and distinguishing it from other ectatic disorders.

---

## Types of Topography

### Placido Disc-Based (Reflective)
- Measures anterior surface curvature from reflected concentric rings.
- Examples: Atlas (Zeiss), Orbscan (partial), Keratograph (Oculus).
- Cannot measure posterior surface or corneal thickness directly.

### Scheimpflug Imaging (Tomographic)
- Rotational camera captures full corneal cross-sections.
- Provides anterior + posterior elevation, pachymetry maps, and keratometry.
- Examples: **Pentacam** (Oculus), Galilei (Ziemer).
- Gold standard for keratoconus detection in pre-surgical screening.

---

## Key Parameters from Pentacam

### Keratometry (K Values)
- **K1, K2:** Flattest and steepest simulated keratometry meridians.
- **Kmax:** Maximum curvature in any meridian — most sensitive KC indicator.
- **Km:** Mean K = (K1 + K2) / 2

### Elevation Maps
- **Anterior Elevation (AE):** Deviation from a reference sphere (best-fit sphere or toric). Values in microns relative to reference.
- **Posterior Elevation (PE):** More sensitive for early KC. Often elevated before anterior changes.
- **Reference surface matters:** Floating BFS gives different values than BFTE (best-fit toric ellipsoid).

### Pachymetry (Thickness)
- **Central Pachymetry (CCT):** Measured at the optical axis.
- **Thinnest Point Pachymetry (TCP):** Often displaced inferiorly in KC.
- **Pachymetric Progression Index (PPI):** Rate of thinning from periphery to thinnest point.
- **Relative Pachymetry Index:** Compares patient's thinnest point to normative database.

### Elevation Indices (Belin/Ambrósio Enhanced Ectasia Display)
- **D value (BAD-D):** Composite discriminant score using AE, PE, thickness deviation, mean K deviation.
- **BAD-D > 1.6:** Moderate ectasia suspicion; > 2.6: high suspicion.

## Common Keratoconus Topographic Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Association |
|---|---|---|
| Inferior steepening | "Skewed radial axis" | Most common KC presentation |
| Nipple cone | Central steep zone < 5mm | Younger patients, earlier onset |
| Oval cone | Decentered, oval steep zone | Moderate-to-advanced KC |
| Globus cone | Generalized ectasia, > 75% of cornea | Advanced KC |

---

## Asymmetric Bow-Tie vs. Keratoconus

Normal corneas show a symmetric bow-tie pattern on axial curvature maps. KC typically shows:
- Asymmetric inferior steepening (I > S by > 1.4 D using KISA% index)
- Skewed bow-tie pattern

---

## ABCD Staging (Pentacam)

Uses four independent parameters:
- **A:** Anterior radius of curvature at thinnest point
- **B:** Posterior radius at thinnest point
- **C:** Thinnest corneal thickness
- **D:** Best corrected distance visual acuity (CDVA)

Each parameter staged 0–4; enables tracking individual component progression separately.

## Normative Reference Values (Approximate)

| Parameter | Normal | Suspect KC | KC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kmax | < 47.2 D | 47.2–48 D | > 48 D |
| TCP | > 490 µm | 470–490 µm | < 470 µm |
| BAD-D | < 1.6 | 1.6–2.6 | > 2.6 |
| Posterior Elevation | < +15 µm | 15–25 µm | > 25 µm |
