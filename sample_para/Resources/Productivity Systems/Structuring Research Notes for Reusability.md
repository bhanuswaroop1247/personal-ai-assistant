# Structuring Research Notes for Reusability

**Type:** Knowledge Reference | **Domain:** Productivity Systems  
**Last Updated:** 2025

---

## The Problem with Disposable Notes

Most researchers take notes that are:
- **Context-dependent:** Only understandable when the original context is fresh.
- **Non-atomic:** Mix project-specific tasks with reusable knowledge.
- **Non-linked:** Isolated silos that can't connect to related knowledge.

The result is a graveyard of notes that are never revisited, losing the cumulative value of years of learning.

---

## Principles for Reusable Research Notes

### 1. Atomic Notes
Each note captures **one idea, one concept, one procedure**. A note titled "Keratoconus and Random Forest" is two separate notes: one on KC pathophysiology, one on RF methodology.

Benefits:
- Easier to search and retrieve.
- Easier to combine across contexts.
- More likely to survive into future projects.

### 2. Write for Your Future Self
Every note should be understandable 12 months later with no additional context. Include:
- Why this matters (one sentence).
- The concept clearly explained.
- Source or reference.
- Any caveats or open questions.

### 3. Progressive Summarization
Capture first, then distill. Forte's technique:
1. **Layer 1:** Raw capture (paste, highlight, quick note).
2. **Layer 2:** Bold the most important sentences.
3. **Layer 3:** Highlight the most important bolded sections.
4. **Layer 4:** Executive summary in your own words (2–3 sentences).

Do Layer 4 only when a note is actively needed — on demand, not in advance.

## Note Anatomy for Technical Knowledge

```markdown
# [Concept Name]
**Domain:** [Field] | **Type:** [Reference / Procedure / Summary]

## What Is It?
[One paragraph plain-English definition]

## Key Formula or Mechanism
[Mathematical expression or logical structure]

## When to Use / Apply
[Context, conditions]

## Common Mistakes / Edge Cases
[What goes wrong and why]

## Reference
[Source, year, URL]
```

## Linking Notes Effectively

Use bidirectional links (in Obsidian or similar):
- Link from specific to general: A note on OOB error links to the broader Random Forest reference.
- Link from project notes to resource notes: The keratoconus paper project links to corneal topography parameters.

This creates a **knowledge graph** — notes become more valuable as more connections accumulate.

## The Review Cycle

| Frequency | Action |
|---|---|
| Daily | Process Inbox → route to PARA |
| Weekly | Scan active Project notes; update status |
| Monthly | Promote useful notes from projects to Resources |
| Quarterly | Archive dormant material; review top Resources |

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Collector's fallacy:** Saving material ≠ knowing it. Notes must be processed and summarized to be useful.
- **Perfect taxonomy trap:** Over-organizing with complex folder hierarchies slows capture. Prefer search + tags.
- **Copy-paste notes:** Re-typed or summarized notes stick in memory; copied text is quickly forgotten.
