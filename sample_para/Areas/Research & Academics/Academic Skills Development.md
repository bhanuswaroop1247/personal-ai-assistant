## Statistical Competency

### Comfortable With
- Descriptive statistics (mean, std, IQR, skewness)
- Hypothesis testing (t-test, Mann-Whitney, chi-square)
- Confusion matrix metrics (accuracy, precision, recall, F1, AUC-ROC)
- Cross-validation methodology (k-fold, stratified, LOOCV, OOB)
- Correlation analysis (Pearson, Spearman, point-biserial)

### Needs Improvement
- **Multiple comparison correction:** Bonferroni, FDR (Benjamini-Hochberg) — important when testing many features
- **Effect size reporting:** Cohen's d, eta-squared — reviewers increasingly require this
- **Confidence intervals for ML metrics** — bootstrapped CIs for accuracy, F1 (currently not in my standard workflow)
- **Power analysis** — needed to justify n=412 sample size formally

### Learning Plan
- Work through *Statistics Done Wrong* by Reinhart (free PDF)
- Implement bootstrapped CI for classifier metrics in paper supplementary

## Scientific Writing Competency

### Strengths
- Methods section writing (clear, reproducible)
- Technical precision in describing algorithms
- Literature synthesis and gap identification

### Weaknesses
- **Discussion section** — tendency to summarize results instead of interpreting them
- **Abstract writing** — passive voice creeps in; need active, impactful sentences
- **Limitations acknowledgment** — I tend to downplay limitations; reviewers will find them anyway

### Writing Goals 2025
- Submit at least 1 peer-reviewed paper as first author
- Attend writing workshop or course (academic writing MOOC)
- Read 5 high-quality Discussion sections from target journals to model tone
