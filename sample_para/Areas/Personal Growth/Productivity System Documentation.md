# Productivity System – PARA + Second Brain

**Area:** Personal Growth  
**Type:** Evergreen – system documentation  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01

---

## System Philosophy

Inspired by Tiago Forte's *Building a Second Brain* (BASB) and the CODE framework:
- **Capture** – collect ideas, notes, references before they're lost
- **Organize** – into Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives (PARA)
- **Distill** – progressive summarization to extract key ideas
- **Express** – produce outputs that build on accumulated knowledge

The goal is not to store everything — it's to ensure that when I need to produce something (a paper, a talk, a solution), I have a rich base of organized material to draw from.

---

## My PARA Implementation

### Projects (active, time-bound)
- Keratoconus Paper → active until submission
- Pupil Tracking App → active until lab deployment
- Interview Prep → active until I land a role

**Rule:** If I'm not actively working on something, it moves to Archive.

### Areas (long-term responsibilities)
- Research & Academics (ongoing throughout MSc)
- Career Development (ongoing indefinitely)
- Health & Fitness
- Personal Growth

**Rule:** No deadlines here. These are standards I maintain.

### Resources (reference material)
- ML knowledge base
- Ophthalmology & Vision Science
- Productivity Systems
- Writing resources

**Rule:** Reference-only. Anything that becomes a project gets promoted.

### Archive (completed or inactive)
- Completed projects, old coursework, past drafts

**Rule:** Never delete — archive. Future me may need it.

---

## Weekly Review Ritual (Sunday, 20 min)

1. **Clear inboxes** (notes app, email, browser tabs)
2. **Review active projects** – any stalled? Any blockers?
3. **Check areas** – any area being neglected?
4. **Set weekly intention** – one key outcome per project
5. **File loose notes** into PARA

---

## Note-Taking Approach

**Capture tool:** Phone notes app (quick capture)  
**Primary storage:** This PARA system (Markdown files)  
**Long-form thinking:** Dedicated `.md` files per project

**Progressive Summarization:**
- Level 1: Captured notes (raw)
- Level 2: Bold key passages (during re-read)
- Level 3: Highlight bold passages (key ideas)
- Level 4: Executive summary written in my own words

## What This System Has Given Me

- Reduced cognitive overhead — not trying to remember everything
- Faster writing — paper sections are assembled from pre-written notes
- Better research continuity — can pick up where I left off after a break
- Career readiness — interview answers come from documented real examples

---

## What Still Doesn't Work
- I forget to do the weekly review consistently (3–4x per month at best)
- Resource notes pile up unread — need to be more selective in what I capture
- Progressive summarization takes more time than I usually invest
