# Health & Fitness – Principles and Current Routine

**Area:** Health & Fitness  
**Type:** Evergreen – reviewed monthly  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01

---

## Why This Matters

Sustained research output requires physical and mental resilience. I've noticed a strong correlation between consistent exercise, quality sleep, and my ability to do deep work. A sedentary thesis period is a false economy — the hours gained from not exercising cost twice as much in reduced focus and increased anxiety.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Consistency over intensity** — A 30-minute walk every day beats an occasional 2-hour gym session. The habit is the infrastructure.
2. **Energy management** — Exercise is not just about health, it's a tool for managing mental energy and stress, which directly affects research capacity.
3. **Sleep is non-negotiable** — No deadline justifies consistent sleep deprivation. Sleep is when memory consolidates, including technical learning.
4. **Don't optimize too early** — Follow a sustainable routine for 3 months before adjusting. Premature optimization leads to program hopping.

## Nutrition Guidelines (Simple, Not Obsessive)

- High-protein meals around training (eggs, chicken, legumes)
- Limit ultra-processed food during high-stress periods (it makes focus worse)
- 2–3L water per day — dehydration is a focus killer
- Caffeine: 1–2 cups coffee before noon; avoid post-2pm (protects sleep)

---

## Sleep Protocol

- Target: 7.5–8 hours
- Consistent wake time: [time] — anchor of the day
- No screens 30 min before bed → replaced with reading (Stoicism, fiction)
- If unable to sleep due to work anxiety: write a brain dump to offload to Second Brain

---

## Metrics I Track (Monthly Check-In)

- Resting heart rate (lower = better cardiovascular fitness)
- Number of training sessions per week (target: 3+)
- Average sleep duration (target: 7.5h)
- Subjective energy and focus rating (1–10 daily, weekly average)

---

## How Exercise Affects Deep Work

I've observed in my own experience:
- Morning exercise → easier to reach flow state in the afternoon
- Rest day + sedentary lab day → significantly worse evening focus
- Poor sleep after late-night coding → next day's problem-solving quality drops noticeably

This is enough evidence for me to treat fitness as a productivity tool, not a luxury.
